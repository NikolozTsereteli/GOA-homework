Hippopotamus



The hippopotamus is one of the largest animals living in Africa. They spend most of their time in rivers and lakes, because their skin can dry out easily in the sun, and the water keeps them cool. Even though they look slow, they are surprisingly fast and very dangerous if threatened. An adult hippo usually weighs between 1,300 to 3,200 kilograms (around 3,000 to 7,000 pounds), making them the third-largest land animal after elephants and rhinos. They are extremely strong, with powerful jaws that can crush almost anything — their bite force is among the strongest in the animal kingdom. Hippos are herbivores; they mainly eat grass, and at night they leave the water to graze on land. A single hippo can eat up to 40 kilograms (88 pounds) of grass in one night. Despite being plant-eaters, they are considered one of the most dangerous animals in Africa, because of their size, strength, and aggressive nature. They live in groups called pods or herds, usually led by a dominant male.
